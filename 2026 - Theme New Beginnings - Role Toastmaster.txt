New Beginnings

Hi everyone,

Theme for today is - New Beginnings.

We're already in February. But I'm still a bit hung over on new year.

Despite not keeping the new year resolutions, I feel we still need new beginnings now and then.

What does it mean by a new beginning? 

I asked my trusted advisor. ChatGPT.

It said - 
A new beginning means choosing to move forward with intention. 
Even though the past still exists.

It’s not about erasing what came before. 
Or pretending you’re suddenly a different person. 
It’s the moment you decide, “I can’t change what happened, but I can change how I respond next.”

A new beginning can be big—like a new job, city, or relationship—or very small, like thinking differently, setting one boundary, or trying again after giving up. What makes it “new” isn’t the situation; it’s the mindset.

Last Monday - there was a Groundhog day. Some of you remember I did a speech on the movie Groundhog Day.

It's a movie where the protagonist or the hero gets trapped in a time loop. He lives the same day again and again.
He starts off as a jerk. In the end, he ends up becoming a decent person.
He does not make any resolution at the mid point or anywhere that I am going to become a better person.

He approaches every day as a new opportunity or a new beginning.
He keeps failing. But he also shows just a little bit more wisdom than the previous day.
In the end, he becomes a better person.

That's our journey too. Or we have a similar journey where we feel we keep reliving the same day over and over - Or we just don't know how to get out of a situation or a problem.

But I believe as long as we get to see a new day - it's a new beginning.

And don't take my word for it.
If you've seen the movie Scarface, there's a line in it that confirms it.
It says "Every day above the ground is a good day."
